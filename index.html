Abstract

Generating talking person portraits with arbitrary speech audio is a crucial problem in the field of the digital person and metaverse. Neural radiance field (NeRF)-based talking face generation systems such as GeneFace has shown success in generalized audio-lip synchronization and high-fidelity 3D modeling of talking portraits. However, there still exist several challenges for GeneFace: 1) it is hard to generate temporally stabilized and natural facial motion given the long audio sequence; 2) the outliers of predicted facial motion occasionally produce bad render results; 3) the slow training and inference speed of the NeRF-based renderer severely obstruct its usage in real-world applications. In this paper, we propose GeneFace++ to address the issues in GeneFace by 1) utilizing pitch contour as an auxiliary feature of the audio2motion mapping and introducing a temporal smoothing loss to improve the time-consistency; 2) introducing locally linear embedding (LLE) projection to regulate the unbounded predicted landmark; 3) designing a computationally efficient NeRF-based renderer to achieves fast training and real-time inference. Extensive experiments show that our method achieves more generalized and high-fidelity talking face generation compared to previous methods.
